# Hey, I'm Juan 👋

Mark Weiser wrote in 1993 that the world is not a desktop.
That the screen is not the center of computing. People are.
That the best technology is the kind that disappears.

I think about that constantly when I'm building.

I'm a Design Engineer based in Buenos Aires with 15 years working
at the boundary where design decisions get messy and frontend decisions
get structural. The interesting problems live there: where a component
architecture choice becomes a product decision, or where an animation
either earns its place or doesn't.

My instinct is to make things recede. Not every interaction needs to be
felt. Not every element needs to declare itself. Good interfaces let
people live in them without noticing the seams.

---

## What I build

Animation and motion, not for decoration, but because that's where
most teams take shortcuts that users register without knowing why.
Spring physics, scroll-driven experiences, systems that stay consistent
at scale.

Design systems: component architecture, design tokens, documentation
that doesn't rot six months after it's written. I've been thinking about
what it means to build systems that AI tooling can actually read and use.

Frontend: React, TypeScript, accessible UI. I care about performance
because slow interfaces break the illusion before the user can name
what's wrong.

Automation: n8n workflows, agentic pipelines, Figma-to-code systems.
These started as internal tooling experiments and became a real part
of how I work.
