## Approach

Mark Weiser wrote in 1993 that the world is not a desktop.
That the screen is not the center of computing, people are.
That the best technology is the kind that disappears.

I think about that a lot when I'm building interfaces.

Not everything needs to demand attention. Not every interaction needs to be
felt. Good design engineering is knowing when to step back, when to let
the interface recede into the periphery so the person can just live in it.

I'm interested in web experiences that are genuinely accessible, honest
about what they are, and built around how humans actually perceive
and move through things.

Craft isn't decoration. It's what's left when the interface gets out of the way.
